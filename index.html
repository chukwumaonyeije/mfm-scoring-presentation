The Documentation Gap

MFM consultation notes are complex clinical documents that must simultaneously serve four distinct audiences — and most notes are not optimized for any of them.

Inconsistent documentation structure makes it difficult to identify recurring patterns, teach residents, or demonstrate note-level complexity for documentation review.
  • Referring OB/GYN — needs a clear handoff with urgency, timing, and next steps
  • Consulting MFM — needs to demonstrate clinical reasoning and complexity
  • Documentation reviewers — need visible MDM support and structured evidence
  • Medico-legal record — needs explicit counseling, uncertainty, and follow-up

Domain 1 · 25 points

Clinical Completeness

The foundation of any defensible MFM note. Evaluates whether a cold reader can reconstruct the clinical context without access to the chart.

Indication

Is the consult question explicit and clinically anchored?

Gestational Age

Is GA stated with dating basis near the top of the note?

Obstetric History

Prior losses, preterm birth, hypertensive disease, anomalies

Medical Background

PMH, PSH, medications, allergies — or explicit "noncontributory"

Symptom Context

HPI with pertinent positives, negatives, and referral reason

Common Deficit

Obstetric history absent or too thin for MFM handoff

06
Domain 2 · 20 points

Risk Stratification & MDM

Evaluates whether the note demonstrates visible medical decision-making — not just data collection, but synthesis and clinical hierarchy.

  • High-risk issues named explicitly (not implied)
  • Numbered, prioritized problem list in the assessment
  • Clinical reasoning language: "raises concern," "consistent with," "warrants"
  • Independent interpretation and counseling are surfaced, not buried
  • Clinical significance of each finding is interpreted, not just described
Most common failure mode: Findings are described accurately but the note leaves the reader to infer what they mean for risk and management.
Fix: "State why each abnormal finding matters and what conclusion you draw from it."
07
Domain 3 · 15 points

Ultrasound Integration & Interpretation

MFM consults are nearly always imaging-driven. This domain checks whether ultrasound findings are documented, interpreted, and linked to management.

5 pts

Findings Block

Key imaging findings in one focused, locatable block — not dispersed across the note

4 pts

Clinical Interpretation

A sentence that converts the ultrasound findings into a management-relevant impression

3 pts

Linkage to Management

Each major imaging finding tied to a corresponding recommendation or follow-up action

Uncertainty Documentation

When certainty is limited, name what remains uncertain and how that changes the plan — especially in early pregnancy viability consultations

08
Domain 4 · 20 points

Assessment & Plan Quality

The section the referring OB reads first and the documentation reviewer scrutinizes most carefully. Structure and specificity drive this score.

  • Problem-based structure: Numbered problems, each with its own plan block directly attached
  • Actionable recommendations: Imperative statements with owner, action, and follow-up
  • Explicit follow-up timing: Interval, responsible service, and escalation triggers
  • Handoff paragraph: Short summary block for the referring team with urgency and next steps
Engine behavior: The engine extracts numbered problem blocks from the Assessment & Plan section and uses them to auto-generate a rewritten A&P that fills structural gaps while preserving the physician's clinical content.
09
Domain 5 · 10 points

Billing & Documentation Optimization

⚠ Documentation-support analysis only — not legal, coding, or billing advice.

  • MDM surface area: Numbered problems + explicit clinical reasoning visible to a reviewer
  • Data review evidence: Prior imaging, labs, outside records, independent interpretation
  • Risk framing: Named maternal and fetal risks with consequence of missed follow-up
  • Structural support: Consistent headings, diagnosis framing, brief MDM summary

Output: Complexity Impression

Each consult receives a documentation_strength rating (strong / moderate / low) and a likely_complexity_impression (high / moderate / low / straightforward) derived from the billing and risk domain subscores.

10
Domain 6 · 10 points

Communication Clarity

A technically accurate note that is poorly organized or ambiguously worded fails at the moment of handoff — when a busy referring provider has 30 seconds.

3 pts

Organization

Stable section headings and tight paragraph structure

2 pts

Conciseness

Note length 5,000–16,000 chars: complete but not padded

2 pts

Ambiguity Control

Follow-up timing + clinical interpretation both explicit

3 pts

Handoff Value

Concise referring-provider paragraph a clinician can act on immediately

A note that earns full marks here ends with a paragraph another clinician can act on immediately, without reading the full consult.

How Scoring Works: Feature Flags

The engine converts raw note text into 30+ boolean and integer feature flags before scoring. No large language model is involved — all logic is deterministic Python.

Clinical Flags

  • has_indication — consult question is explicit
  • has_ob_history — G/P designation or prior pregnancy history
  • has_symptoms — HPI, denials, referral context
  • has_problem_list — numbered assessment blocks extracted
  • has_reasoning — ≥2 reasoning phrases detected
  • has_counseling — counseling content documented
  • has_handoff — referring-provider section present
  • has_followup_timeframe — specific follow-up interval named

Imaging & Risk Flags

  • has_ultrasound_findings — ≥4 sonographic terms detected
  • has_clinical_interpretation — "reassuring," "consistent with," etc.
  • has_linkage_to_management — imaging tied to recommendation
  • has_independent_interpretation — phrase is explicit
  • has_uncertainty_acknowledged — "pending," "possible," etc.
  • high_risk_keyword_count — count of risk-flagging terms
  • has_actionable_plan — ≥4 action terms or ≥3 plan bullets
  • has_diagnoses — ICD-10 section extracted

Medico-Legal Risk Review

Every consult review includes a structured risk flag section across five categories — designed to identify documentation vulnerabilities before they matter.

  • Ambiguity — key findings present but clinical meaning not stated; physician attribution uncertain
  • Weak follow-up language — timing or ownership not explicit enough for high-reliability handoff
  • Missing counseling documentation — counseling content and patient understanding under-documented
  • Poor linkage between findings and plan — major findings not tied to a corresponding action
  • Reduced handoff utility — note lacks a concise referring-provider summary
Each flag bucket either reports a specific issue from the note text, or states "No major issue detected from the extracted text" — never blank, never fabricated.
14

Teaching Feedback

The engine generates a brief, constructive teaching note for each consult — designed to reinforce good habits and correct the single highest-yield gap.

Did Well

Positive Reinforcement

The strongest documentation habit observed in this specific note — drawn from achieved feature flags, not generic praise

Habit to Adopt

Priority Improvement

The single highest-yield change from the top suggestion list — specific and immediately actionable

Habit to Eliminate

Pattern to Break

Either "leaving the reader to infer why an abnormal finding changes management" or "burying the handoff inside long narrative paragraphs"

Tone is constructive, not punitive — the AGENTS.md design principle. Feedback is framed as habit formation, not performance evaluation.

Privacy & Safety Principles

  • All processing is fully local — no data leaves the machine
  • Source PDF files are never edited in place
  • All output stays within the project directory
  • Missing information is labeled as "missing from note" — never fabricated
  • Physician attribution falls back to "Unknown Physician" if not confidently extractable
  • Each source file is SHA-256 hashed for auditability

⚠ Billing Disclaimer

Billing support estimates are documentation-support analysis only — not legal, coding, or billing advice. The output never recommends specific codes or documentation levels.

No OCR

The engine does not perform OCR. If a PDF is image-only, the output warns that extraction volume is low and manual review is recommended.

Getting Started

One-Time Setup

python -m venv .venv
.venv\Scripts\Activate.ps1
python -m pip install pypdf pdfplumber

Run the Workflow

python tools\run_consult_scoring.py

Add new consults, then rerun:

python tools\run_consult_scoring.py \
  --input-dir "Sample patients" \
  --output-dir "output"
